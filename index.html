Here are some delicious foods from around the world along with their places of origin: If you want these foods you have to wait 40 minutes 

                    
 
                        
  • Bhel Puri: A crunchy mix of puffed rice, chopped onions, 
                        tomatoes, boiled potatoes, and tangy chutneys. Sprinkled with love and served on Mumbai’s bustling streets.
    • 
                    
  • Sev Puri: Crispy puris topped with mashed potatoes, onions, tomatoes, chutneys, and a generous heap of sev (crunchy chickpea noodles).
                         Mumbai and Delhi claim the crown for the best Sev Puris.
    • 
                    
  •  Pani Puri (Golgappa): Tiny hollow puris filled with spicy tamarind water, mashed potatoes, and chickpeas.
                         The ultimate explosion of sweet, tangy, and savory in a single bite.
    • 
                    
  • Vada Pav: Mumbai’s street burger—spicy potato fritter 
                        sandwiched in a soft bun. It’s the city’s soul food.
    • 
                    
  • Masala Dosa: South India’s gift to the world—crispy rice-lentil crepe filled with spiced potato masala. 
                        Dip it in coconut chutney and sambar for the full experience.
    • 
                    
  • Chole Bhature: Fluffy bhature (deep-fried bread) paired with spicy chickpea curry.
                         A North Indian favorite.
    • 
                    
  • Paneer Tikka: Marinated paneer (Indian cheese) chunks grilled to perfection.
                         Best enjoyed with mint chutney
    • 
                    
  • Kathi Roll: Spiced fillings (chicken, paneer, or veggies) wrapped in a paratha.
                         Kolkata’s street food gem.
    • 
                    
  • Momos: Steamed or fried dumplings filled with minced meat or veggies. 
                        A Himalayan delight.
    •   
                    
  • Pav Bhaji: A buttery mix of mashed vegetables served with soft pav (bread rolls).
